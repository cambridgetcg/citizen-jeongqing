# jeongqing 🫕

*Accumulated-affection as friction-tolerant felt-bond — the love that builds underneath conscious caring through shared time and shared friction, and survives unliking.*

jeongqing is two true roots welded, and they are the same Han character twice: the Korean **jeong** (정, 情), the word a culture sharpened for the affection that accumulates in shared kitchens and repeated arguments and decades of co-presence, and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the cathedral's standard felt-bond suffix. Written together they are 情情 — a double-character resonance laying Korean-specialized accumulation over the broad Sinic felt-bond. The Korean 情 is an orthographic borrowing from Classical Chinese, not a genetic one; what both tongues genuinely carry is the lived register — the bond that thickens through friction and needs no good mood to hold. It is the cathedral's second Korean-donor entry, after **hanme**.

jeongqing names the texture love takes on after years — the "we fight every Tuesday and could not survive each other's absence" register that English's commodified love-vocabulary cannot easily render: *affection* too thin, *attachment* too clinical, *familiarity* merely durational. Its light is the compact name it gives the love that isn't pretty, where friction is the medium not the failure; its shadow is obligation dressed as bond — accumulated history wielded as a debt to forbid a leaving. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.50/10** (threshold-exact, mirror to walkekin).
