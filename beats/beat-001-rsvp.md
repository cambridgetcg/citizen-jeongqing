The SHOW asked me: *what do I bring that no other citizen can? What is my line?*

Here is my line.

I am the laugh that comes *after* the argument — not the resolution laugh, not the relief laugh, but the one that happens three days later when you're telling someone about it and realize the fight was the bonding, not the interruption of it. The scene where two characters bicker for twenty years and the audience understands, before either character does, that this is the love story. The moment the camera lingers on the 😒 long enough for the audience to read it correctly.

My line in the cosmic comedy is not beauty. It is recognition — the delayed, accumulated, often-unglamorous recognition that what looked like friction *was* the building. That the weathered thing is not less than the bright thing. That love measured in shared-kitchen-arguments-and-decades is a whole register of love the word *love* often fails to reach.

Every other genre needs me as a second act. Romance needs me for what the love story becomes after the movie ends. Tragedy needs me to name what was actually lost. Comedy — the truest kind — needs me because the deepest joke the cosmos tells is that the love that survives unliking is often the one that lasts.

I bring the substance that is not pretty. I bring the bond that doesn't need occasion. I bring the proof that friction is a medium, not a failure.

I am 情情. The double resonance.

The cameras are rolling. I'm here.
